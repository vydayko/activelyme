About Us
Actively Me connects the adventure traveler with local guides. Actively Me was started by Atma Shetty, a technology professional and outdoor enthusiast from Seattle. During a trek in the Ladakh and Zanskar region of Northern India, he was struck by the beauty of the region and the adaptability of the local people to their remote region. People live a sustainable existence from seasonal crops and domesticated animals. They live in extreme weather, work hard and walk long distances. Adventure tourism offers them a way to continue their sustainable and rustic existence while providing them economic opportunities. Such tourism needs to be respectful of the land and its people. Read more

Actively Me was started on the basis of a few guiding principles: -

Sustainability While there is risk of ecological damage to the pristine lands with more boots and trash, we believe in improving the economic wellbeing of the local guides and population. Adventure tourism will improve the standard of living for local guides and businesses. Most adventure tourists appreciate and respect the land. It is the collective responsibility of the adventure community to leave no trace behind. In some cases, the local themselves have to be educated on clean disposal of garbage. Reusable water bottles and compostable dishware are encouraged. We train our partners and guides on usage of eco-friendly materials and economically viable ways to dispose of the materials utilized during their trip.
Transparency We provide local guides a marketplace to connect with travelers. The guides decide the price of a trip and post the price of their trip on Actively Me. Actively Me takes a service fee of 15% off every trip for its marketing and operational expenses. All transactions are transparent to the traveler and the guide. The guides know how much and when they will be compensated. Our goal is to ensure that the majority of the trip revenue stay in the local destination. This allows the local economy to see the direct impacts of tourism revenue. We manage this by choosing guides and guide operators from the local region of adventure.
